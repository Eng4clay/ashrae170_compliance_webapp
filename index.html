Flow calculator — CFM & pressure verification
Manual measurement verification tool — ASHRAE 170-2021 §7.2.1.d
§7.2.1.d: "The room envelope shall be sealed to provide a minimum differential pressure of 0.01 in. of water (2.5 Pa) across the envelope." §7.2.1.e: "Differential pressure between AII rooms and adjacent spaces shall be a minimum of –0.01 in. of water (–2.5 Pa)."
Step 1 — Convert velocity to CFM
Formula: CFM = Average Velocity (FPM) × Area of grille (ft²)
Grille area & CFM result
350.0 CFM  |  Area: 1.00 ft²
Step 2 — Pressure offset verification
Offset result
–50 CFM offset
For negative pressure rooms: CFM Exhaust − CFM Supply = positive offset (exhaust exceeds supply).
For positive pressure rooms: CFM Supply − CFM Return/Exhaust = positive offset (supply exceeds exhaust).
Note: CFM offset confirms airflow direction; differential pressure must be verified with a manometer/magnehelic gauge reading ≥ 0.01 in. w.g. (2.5 Pa) per §7.2.1.d. CFM offset alone does not confirm code compliance.
Step 3 — ACH compliance check
Actual ACH
Formula: ACH = (CFM × 60) ÷ Room Volume (ft³). Room volume = L × W × H.

ASHRAE consultant recommendations

1. Pressure monitoring permanency (§7.2.1.a): Manual manometer readings satisfy interim compliance verification, but the standard requires a permanently installed monitoring device for AII and PE rooms. The BMS feasibility study (3-month milestone) should prioritize these rooms as a code-required upgrade, not merely an improvement.

2. CSSD soiled workroom gap (Table 7-1): The soiled decontamination area requires negative pressure with 2 OA ach and 6 total ach minimum, with 100% exhaust to outdoors. Verify that existing exhaust ductwork discharges directly outdoors with no recirculation, per §6.3.2.1.

3. Laboratory ACH flexibility (Table 7-1, Note f): If lab ACH falls below 6 total ach, the facility must document a formal hazard assessment per AIHA/ASSE Z9.5 to support a lower rate. This assessment must be retained as a compliance document per §4.3.

4. Unoccupied turndown humidity control (Table 7-1, Note ff / §A1.5): All spaces with turndown enabled must have time-delay controls preventing activation within 20 minutes of becoming unoccupied, and humidity control maintaining ≤60% RH during setback.
